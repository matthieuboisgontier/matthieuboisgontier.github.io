Dr. Matthieu Boisgontier is an Associate Professor and the Director of the School of Rehabilitation Sciences in the Faculty of Health Sciences at the University of Ottawa, a Senior Researcher at Institut du Savoir Montfort, and a Senior Investigator at Bruyère Health Research Institute. He is also a member of the Canadian Physiotherapy Association and a certified medical imaging technologist. He received his PhD from Université Grenoble Alpes in France in 2012. His research program blends neuroscience, health, and psychology to improve understanding physical inactivity and sensorimotor control. He has been supported by NSERC, CFI, Banting Research Foundation, MITACS, and FWO. Dr Boisgontier has served on 10+ editorial boards, including Communications in Kinesiology and Peer Community In (PCI) Health & Movement Sciences. His scientific work has been featured in over 50 countries and 500 media outlets (e.g., The New York Times, The Washington Post, The Globe and Mail).
