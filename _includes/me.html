Dr. Matthieu Boisgontier is an Associate Professor at the University of Ottawa in the Faculty of Health Sciences, a principal investigator at the Bruyere Research Institute, a certified Medical Imaging Technologist, a Kinesiologist, and a Physical Therapist.
He completed his PhD in France in 2012 at Universit√© Grenoble Alpes and was a postdoctoral researcher for 8 years in Europe (KU Leuven) and Canada (UBC). His research program blends neuroscience, gerontology, and health to contribute to understanding and solving the pandemic of physical inactivity. He has published over 90 refereed articles that have attracted over 3,500 citations. His scientific work has been communicated to the public in over 50 countries, 500 text media, and 15 broadcast media. He has secured over $2M in external funding as PI, including over $1,350,000 in grants (e.g., NSERC, CFI) and $25,000 from the prestigious Discovery Award from the Banting Discovery Foundation. He has trained over 20 highly qualified personnels, has been invited to give over 15 presentations and lectures, has served on 10 editorial boards including one as Editor in Chief (Communications in Kinesiology), has founded Peer Community In (PCI) Health & Movement Sciences, have reviewed over 250 manuscripts, and have assessed provincial (e.g., FRQNT), national (e.g., CIHR, NSERC, Mitacs), and international grant proposals (e.g., European Commission).
