I am a neuroscientist, a kinesiologist, and a physiotherapist. I am interested in the brain, movement, and health. My MSc focused on the impact of muscle fatigue and sensory supplementation on proprioception and postural control. During my PhD, I investigated the central mechanisms underlying age-related decline in proprioception on the Techniques for Biomedical Engineering and Complexity Management – Informatics, Mathematics and Applications Grenoble (TIMC – IMAG) team that is embedded in the French National Center for Scientific Research (CNRS) and Universite Grenoble – Alpes, France. As a postdoc at KU Leuven , Belgium, and University of British Columbia (UBC), Canada, I explore the brain predictors of postural instability/falls, action selection, bimanual coordination, and automatic tendencies to approach and avoid stimuli depicting physical activity and sedentary behaviors.
